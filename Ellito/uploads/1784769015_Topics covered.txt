Topics covered:

(Tuesday, Feb 3) Axioms for the real numbers. Definitions of the natural numbers, integers, and rational numbers. The square root of 2 is not rational. (Fitzpatrick, section 1.1.)
(Thursday, Feb 5) The distribution of the integers and rational numbers. Inequalities and formulas: triangle inequality, difference of powers formula, geometric sum formula, and binomial formula. (Fitzpatrick, sections 1.2 and 1.3.)
(Tuesday, Feb 10) Sequences and convergence. Definitions, examples, comparison lemma, compatibility with sums, products and quotients. (Fitzpatrick, section 2.1)
(Thursday, Feb 12) Boundedness of convergent sequences, sequential density, closed sets, monotone convergence theorem. (Fitzpatrick, sections 2.2 and 2.3)
(Tuesday, Feb 17) Sequential compactness, definition and basic properties of continuous functions. (Fitzpatrick, sections 2.4 and 3.1)
(Thursday, Feb 19) Extreme value theorem. Intermediate value theorem. Existence and uniqueness of nth roots. (Fitzpatrick, sections 3.2 and 3.3)
(Tuesday, Feb 24) Convex subsets of R, Uniform continuity. (Fitzpatrick, sections 3.3 and 3.4)
(Thursday, Feb 26) Midterm 1 review.

(Thursday, Mar 5) Epsilon-delta continuity. (Fitzpatrick, section 3.5)
(Tuesday, Mar 10) Images and Inverses; monotone functions. (Fitzpatrick, section 3.6)
(Thursday, Mar 12) Limits and introduction to the derivative. (Fitzpatrick, sections 3.7 & 4.1)
(Tuesday & Thursday, Mar 17 & 19) Spring break.
(Tuesday, Mar 24) Computing derivatives -- product rule, quotient rule, chain rule, derivative of inverse. (Fitzpatrick, sections 4.1 & 4.2)
(Tuesday, Mar 31) Mean value theorem and applications. Cauchy mean value theorem. (Fitzpatrick, sections 4.3 & 4.4)
(Thursday, April 2) Review for Midterm 2.
(Tuesday, April 7) Midterm 2.
